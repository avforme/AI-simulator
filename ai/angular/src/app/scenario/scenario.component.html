Well being

Risk aversion. The coefficient of relative risk aversion determines how much risk to take. The lower the coefficient the higher the expected consumption, but also the higher the uncertainty in realized consumption. This is hypothetically illustrated for twenty years by the table and graph below.

RRA coefficient Extra consumption Consumption uncertainty
∞ 0 0
6 27% 19%
3 68% 40%
1.5 226% 90%

When you submit your scenario the near optimal strategy for several different RRA coefficients will be simulated and you can choose the one you prefer.

Risk tolerance. Risk tolerance differs from relative risk aversion. Risk tolerance typically refers to the psychological ability to tolerate losses in investment portfolio value. This psychological risk can be managed by limiting the maximum allocation to stocks. Specifying a value of 100% here doesn't mean the allocation to stocks will be 100%. It may be less depending on the RRA coefficient, how far you are from retirement, and the amount of guaranteed income.

Hard limit on allocation to stocks 5th percentile return 1st percentile return
60% -14% -26%
80% -18% -34%
100% -23% -42%
